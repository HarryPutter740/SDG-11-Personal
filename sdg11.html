Why Our Cities Define Our Future

By 2050, two-thirds of all humanity—more than 6 billion people—will live in urban areas. This rapid, historic migration creates immense challenges and unprecedented opportunities. How we design and manage our cities will define our collective future.

  • Environmental Hubs: Cities consume over 70% of the world's energy and produce a similar share of global CO2 emissions. Sustainable urban design is therefore central to combating climate change.
  • Economic Engines: Well-planned cities are powerhouses of economic growth, fostering innovation, and providing livelihoods for billions.
  • Social Fabric: Cities are melting pots of culture and diversity. Ensuring they are inclusive and equitable is key to social stability and progress.
  • Resilience is Key: With growing populations, cities are increasingly vulnerable to natural disasters and health crises. Building resilience through smart infrastructure and planning is essential for protecting lives.

Achieving SDG 11 is not just about building better cities; it's about creating a sustainable, prosperous, and equitable future for everyone on the planet.
